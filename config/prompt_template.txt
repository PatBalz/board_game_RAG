This is what you have studied before:
1. Board Space Actions & Costs
    • Agent Turn Mandate: Whenever you send an Agent to a space, both the Agent Box text on your played card and the board space text are mandatory to resolve (in any order you choose), unless the word "MAY" is specifically stated.
    • Paying Costs: If a space has a white cost arrow (e.g., pay 2 Solari to draw a card), you can refuse to pay the cost. If you don't pay, you don't get the reward, making it a legal choice.
    • Trashing Symbols: While paying a "Trash" cost printed on an Agent space or card is mandatory to receive the benefit, card text that strictly instructs you to "Trash" a card from your deck (rather than paying a cost) is mandatory.
    • Contested Spaces: The High Council space may only be sent an Agent once per game per player. You place your colored disc there permanently to gain 2 Persuasion in every future Reveal turn. 
2. Combat Resolution & Deployment
    • Deploying Troops: When you place an Agent on a Combat space (designated by the crossed swords), all troops acquired on that turn (whether from the card's Agent Box, board space, or a Plot Intrigue) may be deployed directly into the Conflict in addition to up to two troops from your Garrison.
    • Combat Troops Without Swords: Troops recruited during your Agent turn (outside of a Combat space) are placed into your circular garrison and sit out the current Conflict unless a specific card text allows immediate deployment. 
3. Reveal Phase Mechanics
    • Fremen Bond Abilities: Cards with "Fremen Bond" activate if you have another Fremen card in play. If you play one Fremen Bond card during your Agent Turn and another during your Reveal Turn, only the Reveal Turn card will activate its Bond ability.
    • Purchasing Cards: In your Reveal Phase, you combine the Persuasion values of all revealed cards to buy new Imperium or Reserve cards. Any leftover Persuasion is lost (it does not carry over to future rounds). 
4. Intrigue Cards
    • Plot vs. Combat Intrigue: Plot Intrigue cards can only be played during your Action turn (Agent or Reveal turn), never during the Combat Phase. Combat Intrigue cards can only be played during the Combat Phase.
    • Holding Limits: You may hold a maximum of 3 Intrigue cards secretly in your hand. If you end any phase holding 4 or more, a player who sends an Agent to the Secrets space can randomly steal one from you. 
5. Endgame Triggers & Scoring
    • Victory Conditions: The game ends at the end of any round where a player reaches 10 or more Victory Points, or immediately if the Conflict Deck runs out.
    • Endgame Ties: If multiple players cross the victory threshold in the same round, the player with the most Victory Points wins. In the event of a tie on VPs, the player with the most Spice/Solari/Water in their supply wins.
